PERFORMANCE STANDARD: The learners will philosophically reflect on the lived experiences of Persons with Disability (PWD) through interview and community engagement by evaluating perspectives and social realities, recognizing their own limitations and possibilities for transcendence, and demonstrating prudence, empathy, and social responsibility toward individuals and the community.
Performance Task Title: "Beyond Limitations: A Philosophical Reflection on the Lived Experiences of Persons with Disability"
G – Goal
Your goal is to conduct a respectful and ethical phenomenological interview with a Person with Disability (PWD) or, if direct access is not possible, a parent, guardian, caregiver, or advocate. Through the interview and community engagement, you will philosophically reflect on the participant's lived experiences by analyzing their perspectives, social realities, and challenges. You will relate these insights to philosophical concepts discussed in class and demonstrate empathy, prudence, and social responsibility through a digital ePortfolio.
R – Role
You are a student philosopher, reflective researcher, and responsible community member tasked with understanding the realities of PWDs through genuine dialogue rather than assumptions. Your role is to listen attentively, reflect critically, and communicate respectfully.

A – Audience
Your audience includes:
•	Your teacher 
•	Your classmates 
•	Members of the school community 
•	Community stakeholders interested in promoting inclusivity and understanding of PWDs

S – Situation
Many people form opinions about Persons with Disability without truly understanding their lived experiences. Your class has been invited to raise awareness by documenting authentic experiences and producing philosophical reflections that promote empathy, inclusion, and social responsibility. Your ePortfolio will serve as evidence of your learning journey and commitment to becoming a socially responsible individual.

Phenomenological Interview Questions
(Guide Questions)
These questions aim to understand the participant's lived experiences rather than simply gather factual information.
1.	Can you share your experiences of living with your disability and how it has influenced your daily life? 
2.	Can you describe a moment when you felt genuinely accepted, understood, or valued by other people? What made that experience meaningful? 
3.	What challenges or barriers do you commonly encounter in your community, school, workplace, or public places, and how do you respond to them? 
4.	What gives you strength, hope, or motivation to continue pursuing your goals despite the challenges you experience? 
5.	If you could help people better understand the experiences of Persons with Disability, what message or insight would you want them to remember?
